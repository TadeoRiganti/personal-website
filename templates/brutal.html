    This is a Neo‑Brutalist Website

    It loads instantly. It doesn’t waste your bandwidth. It doesn’t drown you in frameworks, trackers, or bloated assets.

    Like brutalist websites, it’s fast, direct, and unapologetically simple. But unlike pure brutalism, it doesn’t ignore usability. Neo‑brutalism keeps the speed, while embracing the clarity and navigation patterns of modern design.

    What Neo‑Brutalism Means

    • Speed: pages render in milliseconds, no waiting.
    • Structure: semantic HTML, accessible markup, predictable navigation.
    • Mobile‑first: it works beautifully on your phone without hacks.
    • Minimal style: enough polish to feel modern, without excess.

    The Reactionary Spirit

    This isn't just an aesthetic; it's a middle finger. It's a reaction to the homogenization of the web. While you're busy adding a 100kb jQuery UI library just to animate a background color, Brutalism is actually getting the point across.

    "Good design is as little design as possible." — Some German motherfucker.

    Stop over-designing. Look at this shit. It's a website. It has content. You can read it. What else do you fucking want?
